I am an epidemiologist. Following an MPH at Brown University (USA), I completed my PhD at Aarhus University (Denmark). I thereafter joined Steno Diabetes Center as a postdoctoral research fellow. My primary project involves elucidating the trajectories of diabetes-multimorbidity and understanding the dynamic landscape of disease progression. This includes a comprehensive examination of how factors such as socioeconomic status and lifestyle choices influence disease transition states. I am also keen on employing diverse epidemiological methods to triangulate evidence, including causal inference, mediation analyses, and Mendelian randomization.

Beyond the world of data and statistical analyses, I find balance and inspiration through my love of art. I'm particularly drawn to impressionist works.

I believe that good epidemiology, like good art, reveals what might otherwise remain invisible.

I bring strong technical expertise to my research, with proficiency in Stata, R, Mplus, and Git.
